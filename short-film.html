HAK LUEAT

A film by Klumjai Jing Jing — Sound Recordist, Sound Designer & Post-Production Supervisor.

My Roles

Sound Recordist / Sound Designer / Post-Production Supervisor

Team

7 Members

Format

22 min / 4:3 / 24fps / Stereo 2.0 (24-bit / 48kHz)

Date

5 Feb – 15 Mar 2023

Logline

When a female university student suddenly loses the ability to keep any food down, she spirals into a dark, psychological loop of force-feeding and violent vomiting. Desperate for a medical cure, she ultimately realizes her only escape is a chilling acceptance of her terrifying reality.

Mission

Narrative Objective: To construct a heavily claustrophobic and oppressive atmosphere, fully immersing the audience in the protagonist's visceral despair.

Technical Execution: To implement a rigorous file management and editing workflow for a 7-member crew, while maximizing audio recording capabilities to engineer a psychological soundscape that haunts the audience's auditory senses.

My Contribution

This psychological short film explores the human condition when pushed to the absolute breaking point. It reflects the immense pressure of youth, the internal struggle with mental stability, and the physical torment of defying the body's natural limits.

Serving as the Sound Recordist and Post-Production Supervisor, I managed the entire audio ecosystem on set, including boom operation and raw audio file organization. Beyond production, I was responsible for detailed sound logging — designating specific audio cues and Foley for each scene to build a claustrophobic atmosphere.

I also generated comprehensive scene reports and continuity logs to systematically streamline the workflow, ensuring a seamless and highly efficient post-production process for the editing team.
